Introduction

Islamabad is renowned for its serene beauty, characterized by lush greenery, planned infrastructure with wide roads, and a stunning backdrop of the Margalla Hills, featuring iconic spots like Faisal Mosque, Rawal Lake, and Shakarparian Hills, making it a clean, green, and tranquil capital celebrated for its natural charm and cultural sites. Islamabad is surrounded by many tourist spots. One can explore the beauty of nature by visiting places located 45 minutes to two hours away.

Nature spots

Margalla Hills

Hiking trails, wildlife, and sweeping views of Islamabad.

Khanpur Dam

Boating, jet skiing, and picnic spots by the turquoise water.

Murree Hills

Cool climate, pine forests, and the famed Queen of Hills.

Historical sites

Taxila

Ancient Buddhist ruins and a museum rich with Gandharan heritage.

Rohtas Fort

UNESCO-listed 16th-century fort with imposing stone walls.

Adventure spots

Nathia Gali

Lush trails, mountain air, and scenic viewpoints.

Pir Chinasi

High-altitude ridge with panoramic views of Muzaffarabad valley.

Rawal Lake

Boating, fishing, and serene sunsets near the city.
